    The Avett Brothers set out on their 2024 tour, enthralling audiences all over North America from April through November with their brand of folk rock.

    American folk rock group The Avett Brothers hail from Concord, North Carolina. The band consists of Joe Kwon, Bob Crawford, and two brothers, Scott and Seth Avett. The band’s touring members are Bonnie Avett-Rini, Tania Elizabeth, and Mike Marsh.

    Avett Brothers’ 2024 Tour

    The American folk-rock group The Avett Brothers just shared interesting details regarding their 2024 tour. The tour is scheduled to run from April 9 to November 10, visiting different locations across North America in the spring and summer. On January 12, 2024, the band announced on their official Instagram account. Performances by the tour will take place in Detroit, Sacramento, and Columbus.

    During this lengthy tour, fans of the North Carolina-based band may anticipate hearing their music at various locations around the nation. With fresh tour dates announced, more people will get the chance to see The Avett Brothers live, who have become well-known for their distinctive folk-rock style.

    How to Get General Tickets?

    The sale of tickets to the general public will commence on January 19, 2024.

    • Visit the official website of Avett Brothers.
    • On the homepage, locate and click on the “Get Tickets” option.
    • Select the date of the respective tour you wish to attend and click on it.
    • You will be redirected to Ticketmaster’s website.
    • Follow the prompts on Ticketmaster to proceed further and book your tickets for the Avett Brothers concert.

    Avett Brothers

    The Avett Brothers are a folk rock group from Concord, North Carolina, made up of Bob Crawford, Joe Kwon, and two brothers, Scott and Seth Avett. On tours with them also are Mike Marsh, Tania Elizabeth, and Bonnie Avett-Rini. The Avett Brothers, who were once members of the rock group Nemo, combine pop melodies, indie rock, honky-tonk, ragtime, country, punk, folk, and bluegrass to produce a distinctive sound.

    According to the San Francisco Chronicle, their sound combines elements of “the raw energy of the Ramones, the tuneful jangle of the Beatles, the light pop concision of Buddy Holly, and the heavy sadness of Townes Van Zandt.” Their music displays a wide spectrum of influences, with Scott performing banjo, guitar, piano, and kick drum and Seth on guitar, piano, and hi-hat.
