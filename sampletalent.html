    Tim Burton is an American director, producer, animator, writer, and artist, who is best known for his work with films such as “Beetlejuice,” “Edward Scissorhands,” “The Nightmare Before Christmas,” and more. Burton was born in Burbank, California, and when he was only a preteen, he began making short films in his backyard using stop motion animation techniques or film without sound. After graduating from high school, Burton began studying character animation at the California Institute of the Arts in Valencia, California. While studying there, Burton made some of his earliest short films including, “Stalk of the Celery Monster” and “King and Octopus.” After releasing Stalk of the Celery Monster, Burton gained attention of Walt Disney Productions and from there, he began working as an animator, storyboard artist, graphic designer, art director and concept artist for Disney films such as “The Fox and the Hound,” “Tron,” and “The Black Cauldron.” From there, Burton began working as a director for films such as “Pee-wee’s Big Adventure,” “Beetlejuice,” “Batman”, and was the director, producer, and writer for “Edward Scissorhands.” Burton has gone on to work on films such as “Batman Forever,” “James and the Giant Peach,” “Mars Attacks!,” “Sleepy Hollow,” “Planet of the Apes,” “Big Fish,” “Charlie and the Chocolate Factory,” “Corpse Bride,” “Alice in Wonderland,” “Dark Shadows,” “Frankenweenie,” and many more..
