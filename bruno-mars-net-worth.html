    Bruno Mars | Career

    Bruno’s Career skyrocketed after signing up with his mentor, Steve Lindsey. Bruno began writing songs for artists like Adam Levine, Brady, Sean Kingston. He even wrote the famous “Wavin Flag” late performed by K’naan.

    Mars’s first album Doo-Wops and Hooligans, was such a fan favorite that it became a hit worldwide. Songs like “Grenade” and “Just the Way You Are” were being played everywhere.

    The album won him his first Grammy Award a year later. He was nominated for Male Pop Vocal Performance. He also reached a top spot on US billboard Top artists.

    Bruno then starting collaborating with celebrities and musicians.  After that, he released his second album “Unorthodox Jukebox,” which gave the music world hits like “Locked out of Heaven” and “Gorilla.”

    Mars even did some tours after the success of his album before releasing few singles and being featured in the most successful track of the time, “Uptown Funk.”

    We can be aware of how timeless his music style is by looking at people who still jam to “The Lazy Song.”

    Bruno’s recent album “24K magic“ was the most record-breaking of them all. It won him another Grammy with tons of other awards. Mars collaborated with talented rapper Cardi B for songs like “Finesse” on the album.

    His songs with Anderson Paak “Leave the Door Open” became a smash hit and landed up in the Billboard Hot 100. It is from the album Silk Sonic and blew up because of the TikTok dance trends.
