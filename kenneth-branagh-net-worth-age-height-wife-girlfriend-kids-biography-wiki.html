    Early Life & Biography

    Kenneth was born in Belfast in a middle-class family. His father, William Branagh, used to work as a plumber and joiner who also owned a company. His mother’s name is Frances Branagh, and she used to be a homemaker. Kenneth has two other siblings. Kenneth went to Grove Primary School for his initial education.

    Later, his family relocated to Berkshire, England, where he went to Whiteknights Primary School. He got his high school graduation degree from Meadway School. In high school, he was often bullied because of his Irish Accent. He attended the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London and got a degree in acting from there.

    Career

    Kenneth Branagh started his career as a theatre artist and acted in Billy Plays in 1982. He went to the United Kingdom for his performance and won several awards for his on-stage performance. He has worked in movies like ‘Henry V’ in 1989, ‘Othello’ in 1995, and ‘Hamlet’ in 1996.

    From 1989 to 1996, he has directed several movies, including ‘Peter’s Friends, ‘The Magic Flute,’ ‘Sleuth.’ He has also directed the marvel movie ‘Thor.’ His few other notable works are ‘My Week with Marilyn,’ ‘Dunkirk,’ ‘Tenet.’

    He has also directed the Disney movie named ‘Cinderella’ in 2015. He has shared the screen with many notable artists, including Harry Style Emma Thompson. He has worked multiple times with famous Hollywood director Christopher Nolan. He has also directed several movies for OTT platforms and has worked in both TV and Radio projects.

    Awards & Achievements

    Kenneth has been nominated for the Academy Awards five times in five different categories. He was also nominated for the BAFTA awards multiple times. He has received a doctorate from the Queen’s University of Belfast, and the subject was Literature. In 2000, he received the Golden Quill Awards. Next year, he started to serve as a Doctor of Literature at Birmingham University.

    In the year 2009, he lifted the prestigious Lifetime Achievement Award. Later, he became the president of his Alma Mater, which was called the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art. In 2017, the prestigious Free of the City of Belfast title was given to him.
