    Juju Watkins, whose name has reverberated through the basketball community lately appeared on a cover of SLAM and became an emblem for young female hoop stars. So how did she get here, we are going to dive into this young sensation's journey and being on the cover of one the biggest mainstream basketball magazines.


    The Rise of Juju Watkins

    Juju Watkins has already made her presence felt on the court, and she's just 18 years old. How she made her way to the cover of SLAM is a story in itself. Raised in Watts's basketball-crazed neighborhood, she picked up the game early and identified as a prodigy. This is a game that looks as smooth and pro-ready as any out there, so watching her play it will thrill you. Her story is reflective of the changing tides in women's basketball and a harbinger of what could be an exciting new era.


    The SLAM Cover Shoot

    The cover shoot for SLAM magazine encapsulated the essence of this young star. Legendary photographer Atiba Jefferson captured Juju Watkins in her element outside the Galen Center, where she exuded confidence and style. As the sun set over downtown L.A., Watkins served looks while rocking a knitted cropped gray sweater and cargo pants with a pair of Js, giving off major California dreamin' vibes. Being recognized for her talent and presence, she has earned the nickname "The Juju Show," a moniker that truly encapsulates her impact on and off the court.


    The SLAM Feature

    The SLAM feature on Juju Watkins offers an insightful look into her journey and the attention she commands. Her cover crystallizes her L.A. basketball dreams, symbolizing her influence and aspirations within the sport. The feature also includes an iconic "30 Players Who Defined SLAM's 30 Years" section, solidifying her place among the basketball elite. Her meteoric rise to stardom, including a standout performance against UCLA, has garnered national recognition and accolades, cementing her status as a future star in women's basketball.


    Recognition and Support

    Juju Watkins support and recognition crosses large territories. That respect extends across the Association, from giants like LeBron James to homegrown California stars James Harden and Paul George. It is felt around the USC campus as well, where her No. 12 jersey hangs for sale in the school bookstore-proof of the imprint she has left on collegiate basketball.


    What Lies Ahead for Women's Basketball

    This is a big moment for women's basketball and Juju Watkins on the cover of SLAM. Her story, how a prodigy turns into the cover of one of basketball's most prestigious national magazines, is in many ways symbolic of today for women who play sports. Not only is her story one that we can and should support, but it's an exciting tale of hope -- a peek into what the future might look like in this sport.
    As the top of Juju Watkins' basketball world continues to rise, her profile in SLAM is proof that she's no longer a secret and will not be one moving forward. Certainly a hotshot in the world of basketball, with her game resonating with most ball lovers.
