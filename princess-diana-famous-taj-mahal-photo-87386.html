    Get the Real Story Behind Princess Diana's Iconic Taj Mahal Photo - Closer Weekly

    In 1992, Princess Diana famously posed for photographs while sitting in on a white bench in front of the Taj Mahal and today, the snapshot remains one of the most iconic images captured of the late royal.

    23 Aug 2024, 00:00

    In 1992, Princess Diana famously posed for photographs while sitting in on a white bench in front of the Taj Mahal — and today, the snapshot remains one of the most iconic images captured of the late royal.

    RELATED: Kate Middleton Wears One of Princess Diana’s Tiaras — See the Pics!

    But, the stunning photo sadly paints a very different picture of the People’s Princess, as it was taken just months before Diana, then age 30, and Prince Charles announced their separation after 15 years of marriage.

    Princess Diana at the Taj Mahal in February 1992.

    During their official visit to India in early 1992, Diana and Charles, now 67, kept busy with conflicting royal engagements, but were expected to appear at the Taj Mahal together. However, on Feb. 11, Diana traveled to the national monument alone while her husband was in meetings.

    RELATED: Prince William Talks “Painful Experience” of Losing Mom Princess Diana

    When asked by reporters what she thought of seeing the white marble mausoleum in person, Diana replied, “It was a fascinating experience — very healing.” After being pressed to say more, she added, “Work it out for yourself,” according to [People](http://www.people.com/people/package/article/0,,2039522220979410,00.html)_.

    Princess Diana and Prince Charles in India in February 1992.

    Charles — who is now married to Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall — and Diana announced their divorce in August 1996. The Princess of Wales sadly passed away in a car accident just one year later in August 1997.
