    Jane Birkin Career

    Jane Birkin Career: - Birkin made her acting debut in the 1966 films Blowup and Kaleidoscope by Michelangelo Antonioni, both of which featured her in supporting roles. She first met Serge Gainsbourg in 1968 while working together on the film Slogan; this was the beginning of a long-lasting professional and personal partnership. In addition to releasing their debut album as a team in 1969, Birkin and Gainsbourg collaborated on the contentious movie Je t'aime me non plus in 1976. Birkin primarily worked in France, where she had established herself as a great star, but she occasionally made an appearance in English-language movies including James Ivory's A Soldier's Daughter Never Cries (1998) and the Agatha Christie adaptations Death on the Nile (1978) and Evil Under the Sun (1982).
